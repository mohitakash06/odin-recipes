Description

French fries are one of the most popular snacks and side dishes in the world. They are crispy on the outside, soft and fluffy on the inside, and absolutely addictive. Originally popularized in Europe, fries are now a staple in fast food and homemade cooking alike. Making perfect French fries at home requires a simple double-frying technique: the first fry cooks the potatoes through, and the second fry makes them crispy and golden. When seasoned with salt and served hot, they make the perfect side for burgers, sandwiches, or just as a snack with ketchup or mayonnaise.

Ingredients

  • 4 large potatoes (russet potatoes work best)
  • 3 cups vegetable oil (or any neutral frying oil)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ice-cold water (for soaking potatoes)
  • Optional: black pepper, paprika, or garlic powder for seasoning

Steps

  1. Wash and peel the potatoes. Cut them into long, thin strips of even size.
  2. Rinse the potato strips in cold water to remove excess starch.
  3. Place the potatoes in a bowl of ice-cold water and let them soak for 30 minutes.
  4. Drain the potatoes and pat them dry thoroughly with a kitchen towel.

  6. Fry the potato strips in batches for 3-4 minutes until soft but not brown.
  7. Remove and drain the fries on paper towels. Let them cool for 5-10 minutes.

  9. Fry the potatoes again in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 2-3 minutes.
  10. Drain excess oil on paper towels and sprinkle with salt while still hot.
  11. Serve immediately with ketchup, mayonnaise, or your favorite dip.
